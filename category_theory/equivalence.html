Equivalence of categories #

An equivalence of categories C and D is a pair of functors F : C ‚•§ D and G : D ‚•§ C such that Œ∑ : ùü≠ C ‚âÖ F ‚ãô G and Œµ : G ‚ãô F ‚âÖ ùü≠ D. In many situations, equivalences are a better notion of "sameness" of categories than the stricter isomorphims of categories.

Recall that one way to express that two functors F : C ‚•§ D and G : D ‚•§ C are adjoint is using two natural transformations Œ∑ : ùü≠ C ‚ü∂ F ‚ãô G and Œµ : G ‚ãô F ‚ü∂ ùü≠ D, called the unit and the counit, such that the compositions F ‚ü∂ FGF ‚ü∂ F and G ‚ü∂ GFG ‚ü∂ G are the identity. Unfortunately, it is not the case that the natural isomorphisms Œ∑ and Œµ in the definition of an equivalence automatically give an adjunction. However, it is true that

  • if one of the two compositions is the identity, then so is the other, and
  • given an equivalence of categories, it is always possible to refine Œ∑ in such a way that the identities are satisfied.

For this reason, in mathlib we define an equivalence to be a "half-adjoint equivalence", which is a tuple (F, G, Œ∑, Œµ) as in the first paragraph such that the composite F ‚ü∂ FGF ‚ü∂ F is the identity. By the remark above, this already implies that the tuple is an "adjoint equivalence", i.e., that the composite G ‚ü∂ GFG ‚ü∂ G is also the identity.

We also define essentially surjective functors and show that a functor is an equivalence if and only if it is full, faithful and essentially surjective.

Main definitions #

  • equivalence: bundled (half-)adjoint equivalences of categories
  • is_equivalence: type class on a functor F containing the data of the inverse G as well as the natural isomorphisms Œ∑ and Œµ.
  • ess_surj: type class on a functor F containing the data of the preimages and the isomorphisms F.obj (preimage d) ‚âÖ d.

Main results #

  • equivalence.mk: upgrade an equivalence to a (half-)adjoint equivalence
  • is_equivalence.equiv_of_iso: when F and G are isomorphic functors, F is an equivalence iff G is.
  • equivalence.of_fully_faithfully_ess_surj: a fully faithful essentially surjective functor is an equivalence.

Notations #

We write C ‚âå D (\backcong, not to be confused with ‚âÖ/\cong) for a bundled equivalence.
